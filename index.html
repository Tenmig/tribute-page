Here's a timeline of Donald J. Trump's life:

  • 1946 - Born in Queens, New York City
  • 1968 - Graduates from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania with a degree in economics
  • 1971 - Takes control of his family's real estate business, renaming it the Trump Organization
  • 1980s-1990s - Expands his real estate empire, building hotels, casinos, and skyscrapers, including Trump Tower in New York
  • 2004 - Becomes a reality TV star with The Apprentice, popularizing the phrase "You're fired!"
  • 2015 - Announces candidacy for U.S. president as a Republican
  • 2016 - Wins the U.S. presidential election, defeating Hillary Clinton
  • 2017 - Inaugurated as the 45th president of the United States
  • 2017-2021 - Presidency marked by tax cuts, deregulation, Supreme Court appointments, trade disputes, and two impeachments
  • 2020 - Loses re-election to Joe Biden in a contested and controversial election
  • 2021 - Impeached a second time after the January 6 Capitol riot but acquitted by the Senate
  • 2024 - Wins the U.S. presidential election, defeating Joe Biden in a rematch
  • 2025 - Inaugurated as the 47th president of the United States, becoming the second U.S. president to serve two non-consecutive terms (after Grover Cleveland)
